Sapa town stands at the head of a deep valley of magnificent rice terraces that are still farmed today as they have been for centuries. Backdrops don’t get much more spectacular. Enticing ribbons of road lead the eye down to the valley floor, white-water rivers rush among rice fields, and lush green mountains stretch into the distance as far as the eye can see. The highest peak in the region, Mount Fansipan, crowns the ragged ridge line high above town.

Top things to do in Sapa

Summit Mount Fansipan

A 6-kilometre cable car journey will whisk you 3,143m to the summit of Mount Fansipan in just 15 minutes.  Wander the pagoda complex before climbing the last few steps to the summit. The views from the ‘Roof of Indochina’ are unmatched.

Visit an ethnic minority market

Every Sunday the Bac Ha market bursts into life, as hundreds of traders from the Dzao, Han, Xa Fang, Tay and Thai tribes descend on the market, decked out in traditional garb. Expect a riot of colour and excited haggling.

Go trekking in the hills

Sapa offers some of Vietnam's best trekking, and some villages such as Cat Cat and Ta Phin can be seen without a guide. Book an overnight trek to wander through rice terraces, bathe in waterfalls and experience ethnic culture firsthand.

Stay in a mountain lodge 

Stay outside the tourist hub of Sapa in simple comfort at a mountaintop lodge. Sapa is home to excellent lodges and homestays. Set among the rice terraces, you’ll brush shoulders with farmers as they go about their daily lives.

Get active

Mountain biking is a great way to get out and explore lesser-visited corners of Sapa. The annual Vietnam Mountain Marathon attracts some 4000 competitors who run buffalo-beaten trails ranging from 10 to 100 kilometres.

Sapa Weather

The best time to visit Sapa is in September and October when the rice terraces are at their most splendid; or in April and May when the weather is ideal and skies are clear. For those who don’t mind hot weather, June to August is a fine time to visit. Expect chilly weather from November to March.

Sapa Transport

Overnight trains are the most popular way to reach Sapa via the neighbouring province capital, Lao Cai. Upon arrival, transfer vans finish the journey up the mountains. Alternatively, regular buses and shuttle vans run from Hanoi with a journey time of five to six hours, stopping in the centre of town. As a former hill station, Sapa has plenty of steep roads and pathways. Many visitors get around by foot, or by taxi. Adventure-seekers can rent mountain bikes to explore the trails along the valley.

Tourist Attraction

Conquer Fansipan Peak

Fansipan - known as the "roof of Indochina" is always in the top tourist activities that are hard to miss when coming to Sapa. It is known that the most ideal time to conquer Fansipan peak is from September to April of the following year. This is the time frame when the weather and climate in Fansipan is most stable, with little rain and cool.

Trekking Lao Chai - Ta Van

Going about 10km away from Sapa town center to Ta Van commune, you will reach Ta Van village. When you come here, you will feel that this place is completely separate from the bustle of the town. The fields stretch endlessly with no beginning or end, and rustic cottages weave along small roads, creating an irresistible attraction to "feet" that like to move.

Explore Sapa Stone Church

Sapa Stone Church is located in a prime location leaning against Ham Rong Mountain. The church's architecture is in Roman Gothic style with construction materials of carved stone combined with lime, sand and molasses. The details of the dome, roof, and steeple have their own unique features and have never had to be restored.

Join Sapa Love Market

You must have heard many times about Sapa love market - a special cultural feature in Lao Cai, held only every Saturday night. This is an opportunity for young men and women in the village to meet, exchange and learn about choosing their life partner through love songs.

Test your courage at Dragon Cloud Glass Bridge

Located at an altitude of 2,200 meters above sea level, the glass bridge is a real challenge even for the most courageous visitors. In return, you can admire the charming nature of the famous Hoang Lien Son range and enjoy the cool atmosphere.

Local Food

Thang Co Sapa

This is essentially a traditional delicacy of the H'Mong people, nearly 200 years old. Thang Co is usually cooked in a large pan, is dark in color, greasy and has the bitter taste of horse intestine fluid - extremely "suitable" for the cold weather in Sapa.

Kitchen Buffalo Meat

Fresh buffalo meat, after being marinated with spices, will be smoked with smoke from the wood stove until the outer layer of meat is completely dry and turns dark red brown. This is a specialty of the Black Thai ethnic group, possessing a characteristic salty taste and pungent aroma coming from many spices.

Lam Sapa Rice

Lam rice is simply rice grilled in a bamboo tube over charcoal; During processing, the bamboo tube must be rotated regularly so that the rice cooks evenly. Thanks to this special method, the rice grains absorb all the fragrance from the bamboo while still retaining its inherent sweetness. You can eat lam rice with roasted sesame salt or pork sausage, grilled meat... it's all delicious.

Sapa Corn Wine

The natural sweetness of corn, mixed with the aroma of fruit and a bit of Sapa grassland season, creates a unique and characteristic flavor for Sapa corn wine.
